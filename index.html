My Story

Hi, I’m Thomas Spencer. I’m a local web developer based out of Toronto, Canada and I specialize in full-stack application development and design. Whether it’s a new look to a website, or helping build a new application from the ground up, I enjoy the challenge of creating new and innovative applications. I believe that every project I take on has a unique story to tell, and I help turn that story into reality through beautiful designs and code. Although my expertise is in building web applications, my personal passion is in working with people and communities.

Prior to my career in development, I was a social worker in the non-profit sector working with individuals on the autism spectrum and other developmental delays. Although the work was challenging at times, what made it rewarding for me was building a strong community to help foster change and growth for both my clients and my fellow co-workers. I bring these qualities into every project I take on, and embrace its challenges with resiliency and optimism.

Personal Projects

GameTable

View Github Repository

GameTable combines two of my passions: web development and tabletop gaming. GameTable allows users to create sessions for tabletop activities, and allow other users to register to play. This was born from a need at two local game shops in Toronto having issues with managing table space during their weekly events. This application is currently in alpha testing, and will be launching in Q1 2018.

Jaar

View Github Repository

Jaar is a digitalized memory jar! This was a final project designed and created by myself and two classmates at Bitmaker Labs over a two-week period. Using JavaScript, Ruby on Rails and open-sourced gems, we created a fully accessible application that allows users to store, view, and share various multimedia memories within their social networks. We even implemented some voice-activation technology to open and close the jars!

SeatYourself

View Github Repository

A project inspired by OpenTable, SeatYourself was a mid-term assignment at Bitmaker Labs. In collaboration with three other classmates, we created this application over a three-day period mimicking as many features to the original as possible. Our final product received the highest grade in the class, and we were asked to demonstrate our application to our peers.
